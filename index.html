Origami designs: the Art of Folding Paper

Camel

Facts about Camel...

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  3. >amels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Chameleon

Facts about Chameleon...

  1. Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads
  2. Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues.
  3. Chameleons change their colour due to a number of different factors, including their mood,changes in light or temperature,for protection, or the humidity of their environment.

Pigeon

Facts about Pigeon...

  1. Pigeons have strong muscles used for flying. They can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.
  2. Pigeons are herbivores (plant-eaters). Their diet consists of seeds, fruit and various plants.
  3. Pigeons have exceptional eyesight and ability to identify objects on a distance of 26 miles.

Teddy Bear

Facts about Teddy Bear...

  1. The teddy was named after President Theodore Roosevelt, after he refused to shoot a bear during a 1902 hunting trip.
  2. They aren’t just for children – 40% of teddy sales are for adults, and a quarter of us still has our childhood companion.
  3. National Teddy Bear Day is observed annually on September 9th

Panda

Facts about Panda...

  1. A giant panda is much bigger than your teddy bear.
  2. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.
  3. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
